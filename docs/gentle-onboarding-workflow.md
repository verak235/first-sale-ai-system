# Gentle Onboarding Workflow for Moms

Welcome. You are in the right place. This guide will walk you through the First Sale AI System one small step at a time. There is no rush. You do not need any tech experience. Just follow each step when you are ready.

---

## Before You Begin

Take a breath. You do not have to do everything at once. Each step below is short and doable, even on a busy day. Come back whenever you have 15-30 minutes.

---

## Week 1 - Getting Clear

### Step 1: Write Down Your Strengths

Grab a notebook or open a notes app. Answer these questions:

- What do people ask you for help with most?
- What have you figured out the hard way that others still struggle with?
- What could you teach someone in 30 minutes?

Write freely. There are no wrong answers.

### Step 2: Pick One Problem to Solve

Look at what you wrote. Circle one thing that feels most natural or exciting. This is your starting point.

Your idea does not have to be perfect. It just has to be real and helpful to someone.

### Step 3: Describe Your Ideal Customer

Write 3-5 sentences about the person you want to help. Think about:

- How old are they?
- What is their daily life like?
- What problem keeps them up at night?
- Where do they spend time online?

---

## Week 2 - Building Your Offer

### Step 4: Choose a Simple Product Format

Pick one format for your first product. Simple is best.

- PDF guide or checklist (easiest to start)
- Short email course (3-5 emails)
- Short video tutorial (15-30 minutes)
- Template or worksheet

You can always add more later. Start with just one.

### Step 5: Outline Your Product

Write 5-7 bullet points covering what your product will teach or include. Think of it as a table of contents.

Do not write the full product yet. Just outline it.

### Step 6: Set Your Price

A good starting price for a simple digital product is between $9 and $47. Do not overthink this. You can change it later.

Write your price down.

---

## Week 3 - Creating and Setting Up

### Step 7: Create Your Product

Use AI to help you write, outline, or structure your product. You can use a tool like ChatGPT or Claude to:

- Draft your content from your outline
- Edit and improve what you write
- Create a checklist or template

Your product does not have to be long. A 5-10 page PDF that solves one real problem is enough.

### Step 8: Set Up a Simple Sales Page

You need one page that explains:

1. Who this is for
2. What they will get
3. How much it costs
4. How to buy it

Free tools to create this page: Gumroad, Payhip, or Stan Store. All of them handle payment for you automatically.

### Step 9: Write a Short Welcome Email

When someone buys, they should receive a warm welcome. Write 3-5 sentences:

- Thank them for buying
- Tell them what they just got
- Tell them how to get started

---

## Week 4 - Making Your First Sale

### Step 10: Tell Someone About It

You do not need a big audience. Start by telling 5-10 people in your life or online. You can:

- Post in a Facebook group you already belong to
- Share on your personal Instagram or Facebook
- Text a friend who might know someone who needs this
- Post in a local community group

Be honest and genuine. Just say: "I made something that helped me with [problem]. I think it might help you too."

### Step 11: Follow Up Kindly

If someone shows interest but does not buy, it is okay to follow up once. Keep it warm and low pressure:

"Hey, I just wanted to check if you had any questions about [product name]. No pressure at all - just happy to help."

### Step 12: Celebrate Your First Sale

When it happens, take a moment to celebrate. Your first sale is proof that your idea is real, that someone believes in your work, and that you can do this.

Write it down. Remember this moment.

---

## After Your First Sale

Once you have made your first sale, you have a working business. From here you can:

- Ask your first customer for feedback
- Improve your product based on what you learn
- Begin growing your audience slowly and consistently
- Create a second, related product

You have done the hardest part. Keep going.

---

## Getting Help Along the Way

- If you feel stuck, come back to this guide and re-read the step you are on.
- Use AI tools to help you write, think through ideas, or answer questions.
- Connect with other moms going through this same process.

You are not alone. This is a journey, and you are already on it.
